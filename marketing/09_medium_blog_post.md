## The Metrics Your Doctor Uses

In voice science (known as **voice acoustics**), there are four key metrics that clinicians use to assess vocal health:

| Metric | What It Measures | Healthy Range |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Jitter** | Pitch instability (cycle-to-cycle variation) | <1% |
| **Shimmer** | Amplitude instability | <3% |
| **HNR** | Harmonics-to-Noise Ratio (voice clarity) | >20 dB |
| **CPP** | Cepstral Peak Prominence (voice quality) | Higher = better |

These are traditionally measured using **Praat**, a powerful but complex desktop software used by speech-language pathologists and voice researchers. It's free, but let's be honest â€” it has a learning curve steeper than a rock climbing wall.
